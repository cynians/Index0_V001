REFERENCE: MACRO ERAS

POSTMODERNIST PERIOD (≈2024–2090)

Baseline technological and geopolitical world.
Reusable launch systems and large orbital satellite
constellations emerge.


SUPERPOWER WARS (≈2024–2090)

A prolonged period of intermittent global conflict.
The rules-based international order collapses and
numerous geopolitical struggles erupt across the world.

Major powers fragment or reorganize, including the
collapse of the United States, the unification of
Europe into the ENU, the fragmentation of China,
and the emergence of a Pan-African Union.

The wars end with the establishment of the New Order
Treaty, a global collective defense framework that
prevents large-scale interstate war.


RACE FOR SOL (2090–2440)

With terrestrial expansion politically constrained
by the New Order Treaty, geopolitical competition
moves into space.

National and bloc-based space agencies initiate
a prolonged period of prestige-driven exploration
and technological competition known as the Race
for Sol.

Early expansion focuses on symbolic achievements
such as permanent lunar settlements, Mars
expeditions and large orbital infrastructure.

Human presence gradually spreads beyond Earth,
with early settlements established on the Moon
and Mars and exploratory missions reaching
throughout the Solar System.

Although open warfare between major powers
remains politically constrained under the New
Order framework, covert military confrontations
occur in space in a series of clandestine
conflicts later known as the Silent Wars.

Over the course of the era, orbital industry,
planetary settlements and interplanetary
transport networks begin to transform the
Solar System into a permanently inhabited
human environment.

Near the end of the Race for Sol, the first
experimental interstellar missions are attempted,
including early expeditions toward the Alpha
Centauri system.



GGO HEGEMONY PERIOD (2440–5954)

During this era the Global Government Organization
(GGO) consolidates political control over most of
Earth and gradually emerges as the dominant
planetary authority.

The organization develops out of the political
framework established after the Superpower Wars
and the New Order Treaty and evolves into a large
bureaucratic administration responsible for
coordinating planetary governance and major
infrastructure systems.

In its early centuries the GGO remains primarily
an Earth-centered institution. While the
organization exerts influence beyond the planet,
much of the Solar System remains politically
fragmented. Several off-world settlements and
industrial regions maintain their own political
identities or develop into independent power
centers.

The Solar System therefore enters a prolonged
period of uneasy strategic balance between
GGO-dominated Earth and a number of autonomous
Sol-based factions.

Human civilization continues the expansion
initiated during the Race for Sol. Settlements
across the Solar System grow into permanent
population centers, with Mars, the asteroid
belt and several outer system installations
developing into important economic and
political actors.

Early interstellar settlements founded during
the late Race for Sol remain widely separated
due to the vast distances between star systems.
Colonies often reproduce elements of GGO
institutional structures but operate largely
autonomously.

Population management becomes an increasingly
important political concern. Large migration
programs send millions of people to off-world
settlements while Earth itself remains a
densely urbanized world dominated by enormous
metropolitan regions.

Shortly before the First Contraction Event in
the year 3000, construction begins on the COG,
a massive terrestrial population megastructure
designed to concentrate a large share of
Earth's inhabitants.

The unexplained contraction of interstellar
distances triggers a major wave of colonial
expansion. Offworld settlement becomes an
important tool of population management,
sending large numbers of people to distant
systems with varying success.

During the later centuries of the era the
GGO reaches the height of its influence,
exercising considerable political and economic
power across much of the Solar System before
eventually losing its dominance on Earth to
Retronationalist forces.



SECOND MODERNITY (EARTH) (5954–6034)

After the collapse of the Global Government
Organization on Earth, retronationalist states
re-emerge and establish a new geopolitical order.

Although the GGO’s planetary governance structure
disappears, many of the technological and
infrastructural systems developed during the
previous era remain in operation.

Earth experiences a brief period of renewed
nation-state politics, often referred to as the
Second Modernity.



PLANETARY PERIOD (6034–14195)

The Planetary Period begins in the Solar System
with the final collapse of Earth-centered
planetary authority.

Human civilization becomes defined primarily by
independent planetary societies and star system
polities.

In many interstellar systems this transition had
already occurred much earlier. Due to the vast
distances between star systems and the limited
ability of the GGO to exert direct authority far
from Earth, numerous colonies had long developed
their own autonomous political structures.

The beginning of the Planetary Period therefore
represents the formal end of GGO dominance in
the Solar System rather than the universal
beginning of planetary independence across
human space.

During this era the primary political units of
human civilization become individual planets
and planetary systems.

Extensive terraforming efforts and large-scale
biosphere construction projects reshape many
worlds across human space. Organizations such
as the Terran Biome Diversity Project oversee
complex ecological engineering programs.

Advances in communication infrastructure,
including the development of the QuantCom
network, gradually allow distant star systems
to coordinate political and economic activities
across interstellar distances.

Large interstellar trade organizations emerge
during the early centuries of the period, most
notably the Transfer Hansa.

The era ultimately ends with the Alliance
Cascade, a large-scale realignment of
interstellar alliances that results in the
formation of the three major sectors that
dominate the following age.



TRIFECTA DOMINION PERIOD (14195–20219)

The Trifecta Dominion Period begins with the
Alliance Cascade.

Three major geopolitical blocs emerge across
inhabited space:

Gold Sector
Blue Sector
Rust Sector

Interstellar politics becomes defined by the
interaction and rivalry between these three
large regional powers.

Planetary states increasingly align themselves
with one of the three sectors while retaining
varying degrees of autonomy.

Large institutions originating in the
Planetary Period continue to operate during
this era, though often in diminished roles.

Terraforming and planetary settlement continue
throughout the period as sector civilizations
expand into new regions of space.

The era ultimately gives way to a new diplomatic
order with the creation of the Intersector
Assembly.



INTERSECTOR ASSEMBLY PERIOD (20219–35101)

A diplomatic order coordinating relations
between the three sectors and managing
conflicts between their expanding spheres
of influence.

IDEA-227

Rust Sector Industrial Architecture

spawned-from: Rust Sector Manufacturing Culture

Manufacturing plants constructed across the Rust Sector
develop a distinctive architectural style.

Core characteristics:

- angular reinforced concrete structures
- low-profile, stealth-like geometries
- minimal windows
- heavy cargo gates and exterior pipe corridors
- modular expansion joints
- oxidized metal function bands around outer walls

Plant complexes usually follow a radial hub layout.

A central industrial core contains the primary production
process, while long linear production arms extend outward
from the hub.

Facilities often grow organically as new arms are added
over time, producing star-shaped industrial complexes
visible from orbit.

IDEA-229
------------------------------------------------

Keggel Class – Hansa Bulk Carrier

spawned-from: Transfer Hansa Logistics Fleet

The Keggel Class Hansa Bulk Carrier is a purpose-built
system-level bulk container transport vessel used
throughout the Transfer Hansa trade network.

The design philosophy follows a common Hansa logistics
principle:

large interstellar carrier ships move fleets of smaller
system-autonomous delivery vessels.

The Keggel Class operates only within star systems.

Characteristics:

- optimized for high-volume container transport
- minimal crew requirements
- long endurance system-level propulsion
- modular container docking structures
- simple, robust construction

For interstellar travel, Keggel Class carriers dock to
much larger Transfer Hansa interstellar freight ships
which transport entire groups of system carriers between stars.

The class was produced in immense numbers and became one
of the most common cargo vessels in the Hansa network.

Name: Oxyde System
Domain: Systems

Parent Idea: None

Era:
Planetary Period
Trifecta Dominion Period
Rust Sector Revolution

Tags:
system
industry
metal-rich
rust-sector

Description:
Metal-rich stellar system orbiting a bright red dwarf star.

The system formed from unusually metal-dense nebular material,
making its planets and asteroid bodies rich in industrial metals.

Because the habitable zone lies close to the star, industrial
activity concentrated in the inner system.

During the Planetary Period multiple corporations established
large-scale industrial infrastructure in the system, including
orbital shipyards, metallurgical processing stations, and asteroid
extraction networks.

The primary industrial world became Patina.

During the late Planetary Period, corporate tensions escalated
into a corporate and mercenary war in the system.

Much of Patina's orbital infrastructure was destroyed during
the conflict.

After the collapse of corporate authority, Rust Sector forces
stabilized the system and Patina declared independence from
the Gold Sector.

The system subsequently joined the Rust Sector faction.

Description:
Industrial production world in the Oxyde System.

Patina was originally selected as a Terran Biome Diversity
Project (BDP) terraforming world and seeded using an early
ark-seed colonization method.

These slow interstellar missions assumed long isolation
periods during which terraforming would gradually stabilize
the planetary environment.

arcologies, and orbital ship construction infrastructure.

IDEA-236
------------------------------------------------

Name: Patina Omni-Production Shipyard
Domain: Production

Parent Idea:
IDEA-231 Planet Patina
IDEA-233 Capital Pursuits

Era:
Planetary Period
Late Planetary Period

Tags:
shipyard
ai-designed
industrial
production

Description:
Large orbital ship construction complex originally built in
orbit around Patina during the corporate expansion of the
Planetary Period.

The facility was commissioned by the frontier speculative
corporation Capital Pursuits and designed using an early
generative industrial design AI.

Instead of a traditional shipyard layout, the facility grew
along a central structural spine called the main bus.

This bus carried power conduits, logistics rails, structural
trusses, and data networks.

Whenever production capacity increased, the AI attached
additional fabrication modules to the spine.

Over time the complex expanded into a massive irregular
network of assembly frames, welding arms, fabrication pods,
cargo manipulators, and docking structures.

Repeated modifications by engineers and management created
large amounts of industrial control "spaghetti code".

Although inefficient and difficult to maintain, the shipyard
remained extremely productive due to the Oxyde system's
abundant metal resources and permissive industrial environment.

The facility became one of the largest spacecraft construction
sites in the system.

Large portions of the structure were destroyed during the
corporate and mercenary war of the late Planetary Period and
now drift in the Patina Orbital Graveyard.

Description:
Large modular interstellar cargo carrier architecture
developed at the Patina Omni-Production Shipyard.

The Porcupine consists of a long central truss structure
equipped with docking rails along its entire length.

System-scale container spine freighters can attach directly
to the structure.

When assembled for interstellar travel dozens of freighters
dock radially along the spine, producing a dense star-like

During transit the attached freighters function as cargo

Upon arrival in another system the freighters detach and
resume local cargo distribution.

The Porcupine architecture allowed industrial systems like
Oxyde to move enormous volumes of manufactured goods between
star systems.

IDEA-250
------------------------------------------------

Name: BDP Terraforming Phases
Domain: Terraforming

Parent Idea: None

Era:
Planetary Period
Trifecta Dominion Period

Tags:
terraforming
bdp
ecology
planetary_engineering

Description:
Standardized terraforming methodology used by the Terran
Biome Diversity Project (BDP). Instead of introducing full
Earth ecosystems at once, planetary biospheres are constructed
gradually in sequential ecological phases to stabilize planetary
biogeochemical cycles.

Phase 1 — Planetary Stabilization
Atmospheric regulation, microbial biosphere introduction,
hydrological cycle formation, ocean chemistry stabilization,
and temperature balancing.

Phase 2 — Structural Ecosystems
Introduction of organisms that physically structure environments
such as reef-building organisms, kelp forests, marsh vegetation,
microbial mats, and soil-forming plant systems.

Phase 3 — Primary Ecological Web
Introduction of herbivores, plankton systems, detritivores,
reef grazers, and small omnivores to stabilize nutrient cycles.

Phase 4 — Predator Layer
Predators are introduced after lower trophic layers demonstrate
long-term stability. These regulate population dynamics.

Phase 5 — Biodiversity Expansion
Expansion of ecological complexity through specialized niches,
rare species, and complex predator–prey interactions.

The Bolt Class is a Rust Sector interstellar cargo and infiltration vessel
characterized by an extremely narrow needle-like hull designed to minimize
the probability of micro-collisions during interstellar transit.

The ship resembles a long screw or needle rather than a conventional wide
spacecraft. This geometry reduces the forward cross-section exposed to
high-velocity dust particles encountered between star systems.

The vessel is built around a central structural spine ending in a reinforced
needle tip containing impact shielding and forward sensors.

Cargo and mission modules attach externally along the central span while
maintaining a very small forward profile. Some modules function as detachable
infiltration pods capable of independent operations.

During interstellar travel the vessel rotates along its longitudinal axis,
spreading micro-impact wear across the hull while generating partial
artificial gravity.

Because of the narrow hull the ship cannot produce full Earth gravity.
The rear crew habitat section, where the diameter is largest, reaches
approximately 0.7 g.

The ship employs a hybrid propulsion architecture consisting of a deployable
lightsail mounted along the central spine for interstellar acceleration and
large ion engines used for deceleration and maneuvering within star systems.

The vessel also features docking ports for Rust Sector Symbiont Ships,
smaller spacecraft that attach using standardized interface modules.

Description:
The Antonia Experiment is a Cultural Seeding Group (CSG) planetary civilization experiment designed to study whether societies based on artificial historical myths can evolve into stable political cultures.

The Seeding Approach Paper specifies the controlled creation of several civilizations modeled on cultures of the ancient Mediterranean world. The primary subject is the Antonian Republic, a society designed to reproduce the political and cultural structures of the Late Roman Republic (approximately 100–30 BC).

The Antonian population was seeded with an implanted historical narrative describing ancient republican traditions, legendary founders, aristocratic family lineages, and long-standing political institutions. Although fictional, this narrative provides the cultural framework through which the population understands its society.

Technological development was restricted to a near-authentic Roman technological level including iron metallurgy, animal-powered agriculture, sailing vessels, chariot transport, and masonry construction. Only minor stabilizing improvements were introduced such as improved crop strains and marginally enhanced metallurgy.

To reproduce a Mediterranean geopolitical ecosystem the experiment also seeded neighboring civilizations including Greek-style maritime city states, an Egyptian-style river monarchy whose administrative elite speaks Ptolemaic Greek, and a Persian-style imperial civilization located on another continent of the planet.

The experiment observes the evolution of republican institutions, aristocratic competition, patron–client networks, military prestige politics, and the long-term stability of myth-based historical identity.

Description:
The Antonian Republic is the primary civilization seeded in the Antonia cultural experiment and represents a close reconstruction of the political and social structures of the Late Roman Republic.

The state is organized as a senatorial republic governed by aristocratic families whose members dominate the senate. The senate directs foreign policy, finances, and military command while elected magistrates exercise executive authority for limited terms.

Citizen assemblies formally elect magistrates and approve legislation, though political influence is heavily shaped by patronage networks maintained by elite families.

Successful generals gain enormous prestige and political influence, creating structural tensions between republican institutions and ambitious military leaders.

Religion follows the Roman civic religious system including temples, priesthoods, public festivals, and ritual ceremonies reinforcing the authority of the republic.

Description:
The Superpower Wars were a prolonged period of intermittent global
conflict lasting roughly from the mid-2020s until the end of the 21st
century.

The wars began with a large-scale conflict in Eastern Europe involving
Russia and Belarus against NATO members without direct initial
participation by the United States.

Shortly afterwards China launched an invasion of Taiwan, drawing the
United States into open conflict in the Pacific theater.

Over the following decades numerous additional regional conflicts
erupted around the world as long-suppressed geopolitical disputes
resurfaced.

The weakening of international institutions and the collapse of the
post–Cold War rules-based order allowed many states and regional
powers to pursue territorial ambitions or settle historic rivalries
through military force.

The wars therefore consisted not of a single continuous global
conflict but of overlapping wars, ceasefires, proxy conflicts and
renewed escalations across multiple theaters over nearly a century.

By the end of the era several states had collapsed or disappeared,
global political structures had been radically transformed, and the
world entered a new geopolitical configuration.

The devastation and instability of this period ultimately contributed
to a growing political desire to redirect human competition away from
Earth and toward expansion into space, laying the psychological and
political foundations for the Race for Sol.

IDEA-374
------------------------------------------------

Name: The New Order Treaty

Domain: Geopolitics

Parent Idea:
IDEA-372 Superpower Wars

Era:
Superpower Wars

Tags:
treaty
collective-defense
postwar-order
geopolitics

Description:
The New Order Treaty is the international security framework that
emerged at the end of the Superpower Wars.

After nearly a century of intermittent global conflict, the surviving
major political blocs sought to prevent another large-scale interstate
war. The resulting treaty established a system combining collective
defense guarantees, strict recognition of sovereign borders, and
limitations on large-scale conventional military offensives between
signatory states.

Under the treaty an attack on any participating sovereign state would
be treated as an attack on all members of the agreement, creating an
inverted alliance structure designed to make open interstate warfare
politically and militarily unviable.

Although the treaty dramatically reduced the likelihood of large
conventional wars, covert operations, proxy conflicts and intelligence
competition between major powers continued to occur.

Crucially, the treaty does not regulate or restrict expansion beyond
Earth. Outer space remained outside the jurisdiction of the New Order
framework.

This omission unintentionally redirected geopolitical competition away
from terrestrial conquest and toward extraterrestrial expansion,
triggering the surge of exploration and settlement efforts that
became known as the Race for Sol.

into the Solar System, beginning shortly after the establishment of the
New Order Treaty in the late 21st century.

Because the New Order framework effectively prevented territorial
expansion on Earth, geopolitical competition between the surviving
power blocs increasingly shifted toward space.

National space agencies and newly formed political coalitions began
rapidly expanding their space programs, initiating a prolonged period
of technological development, exploration and settlement throughout
the Solar System.

Early spacecraft designs during the first centuries of the era still
closely resembled late Postmodernist launch vehicles and spacecraft,
relying on large chemical rockets and modular space stations.

The first permanent human footholds were established on the Moon,
followed by early settlements on Mars. Over time human presence spread
to additional planetary bodies including asteroids, orbital habitats
and outer planetary systems.

Orbital manufacturing and large-scale space industry gradually emerged
as economically viable sectors, producing specialized materials and
components that could not easily be manufactured on Earth.

Although open warfare between major powers remained politically
dangerous under the New Order Treaty, covert military operations
in space occurred throughout the era in a series of clandestine
confrontations later referred to as the Silent Wars.

Despite these tensions, the overall human presence within the Solar
System grew steadily throughout the Race for Sol, transforming space
from a frontier of exploration into a permanently inhabited human
environment.

Description:
The Lübeck Class Shellship is one of the earliest successful interstellar convoy carriers used during the formative expansion of the Transfer Hansa.

Measuring roughly 2.5 kilometers in length, Lübeck Class vessels transport fleets of merchant ships between nearby star systems.

The ship is built around a large cylindrical carrier body forming a hollow docking tunnel running through most of the vessel’s length. This tunnel is not pressurized and remains open to vacuum.

The rear of the tunnel is open, allowing merchant vessels to enter directly from behind the ship during docking operations.

Inside the carrier tunnel, ships attach to modular docking platforms connected to an internal rail logistics system. These platforms allow vessels to be repositioned within the tunnel according to unloading order or cargo configuration.

Most ships are carried inside the tunnel, while exceptionally large bulk carriers attach to reinforced external hardpoints along the hull.

A typical convoy configuration includes roughly 15 heavy bulk carriers, around 80 medium freighters, and numerous smaller trader vessels.

The outer hull surrounding the carrier tunnel contains life-support systems, engineering sections, cargo processing facilities, and habitation areas. Some of these habitation sections rotate to provide artificial gravity.

Propulsion is provided by a large engine block mounted behind the carrier body and connected by heavy thrust trusses.

The Lübeck Class enabled the rapid expansion of early Transfer Hansa interstellar trade routes.
