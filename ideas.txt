REFERENCE: MACRO ERAS
================================================

POSTMODERNIST PERIOD

Late industrial and early space age civilization on Earth.
Geopolitical tensions culminate in the Superpower Wars.


RACE FOR SOL

Human expansion into the Solar System.
Major colonization of Mars, the Moon, and other Sol bodies.


GGO HEGEMONY PERIOD

The Global Government Organization consolidates large parts of
Earth and attempts to extend influence across Sol and early
interstellar colonies.


PLANETARY PERIOD

Large numbers of planets are terraformed and settled.
Independent planetary societies emerge across interstellar space.


TRIFECTA DOMINION PERIOD

Three geopolitical blocs dominate interstellar politics:

Gold Sector
Blue Sector
Rust Sector


INTERSECTOR ASSEMBLY

Later diplomatic order coordinating relations between sectors.

IDEA-226

Rust Sector Culture: Oxidation Bands

spawned-from: Rust Sector Industrial Architecture

Rust Sector industrial buildings often feature a distinctive
oxidized metal band running around the outer wall of the
structure.

Characteristics:

- angular concrete buildings
- minimal windows
- stealth-like industrial geometry
- oxidized metal band encircling the structure

The oxidation band serves both cultural and practical purposes.

Functions:

- indicates facility function
- indicates plant age and expansion phase
- acts as a visual industrial classification system
- becomes a recognizable cultural marker of Rust Sector industry

IDEA-227
------------------------------------------------

Rust Sector Industrial Architecture

spawned-from: Rust Sector Manufacturing Culture

Manufacturing plants constructed across the Rust Sector
develop a distinctive architectural style.

Core characteristics:

- angular reinforced concrete structures
- low-profile, stealth-like geometries
- minimal windows
- heavy cargo gates and exterior pipe corridors
- modular expansion joints
- oxidized metal function bands around outer walls

Plant complexes usually follow a radial hub layout.

A central industrial core contains the primary production
process, while long linear production arms extend outward
from the hub.

Facilities often grow organically as new arms are added
over time, producing star-shaped industrial complexes
visible from orbit.

IDEA-229
------------------------------------------------

Keggel Class – Hansa Bulk Carrier

spawned-from: Transfer Hansa Logistics Fleet

The Keggel Class Hansa Bulk Carrier is a purpose-built
system-level bulk container transport vessel used
throughout the Transfer Hansa trade network.

The design philosophy follows a common Hansa logistics
principle:

large interstellar carrier ships move fleets of smaller
system-autonomous delivery vessels.

The Keggel Class operates only within star systems.

Characteristics:

- optimized for high-volume container transport
- minimal crew requirements
- long endurance system-level propulsion
- modular container docking structures
- simple, robust construction

For interstellar travel, Keggel Class carriers dock to
much larger Transfer Hansa interstellar freight ships
which transport entire groups of system carriers between stars.

The class was produced in immense numbers and became one
of the most common cargo vessels in the Hansa network.

IDEA-230
------------------------------------------------

Name: Oxyde System
Domain: Systems

Parent Idea: None

Era:
Planetary Period
Trifecta Dominion Period
Rust Sector Revolution

Tags:
system
industry
metal-rich
rust-sector

Description:
Metal-rich stellar system orbiting a bright red dwarf star.

The system formed from unusually metal-dense nebular material,
making its planets and asteroid bodies rich in industrial metals.

Because the habitable zone lies close to the star, industrial
activity concentrated in the inner system.

During the Planetary Period multiple corporations established
large-scale industrial infrastructure in the system, including
orbital shipyards, metallurgical processing stations, and asteroid
extraction networks.

The primary industrial world became Patina.

During the late Planetary Period, corporate tensions escalated
into a corporate and mercenary war in the system.

Much of Patina's orbital infrastructure was destroyed during
the conflict.

After the collapse of corporate authority, Rust Sector forces
stabilized the system and Patina declared independence from
the Gold Sector.

The system subsequently joined the Rust Sector faction.

IDEA-231
------------------------------------------------

Name: Planet Patina
Domain: Locations

Parent Idea:
IDEA-230 Oxyde System

Era:
Planetary Period
Trifecta Dominion Period
Rust Sector Revolution

Tags:
planet
industrial-world
bdp
terraforming

Description:
Industrial production world in the Oxyde System.

Patina was originally selected as a Terran Biome Diversity
Project (BDP) terraforming world and seeded using an early
ark-seed colonization method.

These slow interstellar missions assumed long isolation
periods during which terraforming would gradually stabilize
the planetary environment.

However, rapid advances in interstellar propulsion during the
Planetary Period allowed corporate expansion fleets to reach
the Oxyde System before the terraforming process was complete.

Corporate prospectors discovered a partially terraformed world
in a metal-rich system and converted the planet into a heavy
industrial production center.

Terraforming efforts were gradually abandoned while the
planet developed large metallurgical complexes, factory
arcologies, and orbital ship construction infrastructure.

The planet's name reflects the oxidized industrial landscapes
that dominate its surface.

IDEA-232

Name: Patina Orbital Graveyard
Domain: Locations

Parent Idea:
IDEA-231 Planet Patina

Era:
Late Planetary Period
Trifecta Dominion Period

Tags:
orbital
salvage
wreck-field
shipyard

Description:
Large orbital debris region surrounding Patina.

The graveyard formed during the corporate and mercenary war
of the late Planetary Period when large portions of Patina's
orbital infrastructure were destroyed.

The debris field consists of several distinct orbital bands.

Shipyard Ruin Belts:
Destroyed orbital shipyards, fabrication frames, and partially
completed spacecraft hulls drifting in large skeletal structures.

Battlefield Debris Fields:
Clusters of wrecked corporate fleet vessels and mercenary
combat ships remaining from the orbital battles.

Residual Logistics Orbits:
Smaller regions containing abandoned cargo infrastructure
from the earlier corporate dominance period, including
cargo containers, docking frameworks, and transfer stations.

The graveyard later became the center of the system's
salvage economy.

IDEA-236
------------------------------------------------

Name: Patina Omni-Production Shipyard
Domain: Production

Parent Idea:
IDEA-231 Planet Patina
IDEA-233 Capital Pursuits

Era:
Planetary Period
Late Planetary Period

Tags:
shipyard
ai-designed
industrial
production

Description:
Large orbital ship construction complex originally built in
orbit around Patina during the corporate expansion of the
Planetary Period.

The facility was commissioned by the frontier speculative
corporation Capital Pursuits and designed using an early
generative industrial design AI.

Instead of a traditional shipyard layout, the facility grew
along a central structural spine called the main bus.

This bus carried power conduits, logistics rails, structural
trusses, and data networks.

Whenever production capacity increased, the AI attached
additional fabrication modules to the spine.

Over time the complex expanded into a massive irregular
network of assembly frames, welding arms, fabrication pods,
cargo manipulators, and docking structures.

Repeated modifications by engineers and management created
large amounts of industrial control "spaghetti code".

Although inefficient and difficult to maintain, the shipyard
remained extremely productive due to the Oxyde system's
abundant metal resources and permissive industrial environment.

The facility became one of the largest spacecraft construction
sites in the system.

Large portions of the structure were destroyed during the
corporate and mercenary war of the late Planetary Period and
now drift in the Patina Orbital Graveyard.

Name: Oxyde Container Spine Freighter
Domain: Vehicles

Parent Idea:

Era:
Planetary Period
Late Planetary Period

Tags:
freighter
cargo

logistics

Description:
Mass-produced intra-system cargo freighter built at the
Patina Omni-Production Shipyard.

The vessel consists primarily of a long structural truss
spine equipped with standardized container clamps.

Cargo containers are mounted externally along the spine
rather than enclosed within a hull.

This configuration made the ships inexpensive to manufacture,
easy to repair, and highly adaptable to industrial logistics.

facilities throughout the Oxyde system.

Thousands were produced during the system's corporate

The freighters were also designed to dock onto large
interstellar carrier structures known as Patina Porcupines.

IDEA-238
------------------------------------------------

Name: Patina Porcupine Carrier Architecture
Domain: Vehicles

Parent Idea:
IDEA-236 Patina Omni-Production Shipyard

Era:
Planetary Period
Late Planetary Period

Tags:
interstellar
carrier
cargo
modular

Description:
Large modular interstellar cargo carrier architecture
developed at the Patina Omni-Production Shipyard.

The Porcupine consists of a long central truss structure
equipped with docking rails along its entire length.

System-scale container spine freighters can attach directly
to the structure.

When assembled for interstellar travel dozens of freighters
dock radially along the spine, producing a dense star-like
shape resembling a porcupine or sea urchin.

During transit the attached freighters function as cargo
modules while the carrier provides propulsion and navigation.

Upon arrival in another system the freighters detach and
resume local cargo distribution.

The Porcupine architecture allowed industrial systems like
Oxyde to move enormous volumes of manufactured goods between
star systems.

IDEA-239
------------------------------------------------

Name: Porcupine Engine Module System
Domain: Technology / Logistics

Parent Idea:
IDEA-238 Patina Porcupine Carrier Architecture

Era:
Planetary Period
Late Planetary Period

Tags:
propulsion
interstellar
modular
transport

Description:
Propulsion architecture used by Patina Porcupine carriers.

The Porcupine itself is only the structural truss carrier.

Interstellar propulsion is provided by a detachable engine
module mounted at one end of the structure.

The engine module contains the interstellar drive system,
navigation core, communication arrays, and primary power
generation systems.

Because the propulsion unit is detachable, Porcupine carriers
are identified by the name of their engine module.

Standard designation format:

PP - Engine Name

Examples:

PP - Iron Wake
PP - Solar Hinge
PP - Red Transit
PP - Foundry Flame

Engine modules can be replaced or refitted while the truss
carrier continues operating.

Description:
Narrow orbital debris bands surrounding Patina.

systems used during the industrial operation of the Patina
shipyards.

pushed into designated orbital lanes to keep shipping routes

IDEA-314
------------------------------------------------
Name:
SAP – Antonia Experiment

Domain:
Science & Research

Parent Idea:
Cultural Seeding Group

Era:
Planetary Period

Tags:
sap
csg
civilization_experiment
rome
mediterranean

Description:
The Antonia Experiment is a Cultural Seeding Group (CSG) planetary civilization experiment designed to study whether societies based on artificial historical myths can evolve into stable political cultures.

The Seeding Approach Paper specifies the controlled creation of several civilizations modeled on cultures of the ancient Mediterranean world. The primary subject is the Antonian Republic, a society designed to reproduce the political and cultural structures of the Late Roman Republic (approximately 100–30 BC).

The Antonian population was seeded with an implanted historical narrative describing ancient republican traditions, legendary founders, aristocratic family lineages, and long-standing political institutions. Although fictional, this narrative provides the cultural framework through which the population understands its society.

Technological development was restricted to a near-authentic Roman technological level including iron metallurgy, animal-powered agriculture, sailing vessels, chariot transport, and masonry construction. Only minor stabilizing improvements were introduced such as improved crop strains and marginally enhanced metallurgy.

To reproduce a Mediterranean geopolitical ecosystem the experiment also seeded neighboring civilizations including Greek-style maritime city states, an Egyptian-style river monarchy whose administrative elite speaks Ptolemaic Greek, and a Persian-style imperial civilization located on another continent of the planet.

The experiment observes the evolution of republican institutions, aristocratic competition, patron–client networks, military prestige politics, and the long-term stability of myth-based historical identity.

IDEA-315
------------------------------------------------
Name:
Antonia

Domain:
Planets

Parent Idea:
Antonia Experiment

Era:
Planetary Period

Tags:
csg
experimental_world
mediterranean
civilization_seed

Description:
Antonia is a Cultural Seeding Group experimental world designed to host several civilizations modeled after societies of the ancient Mediterranean basin.

The planet’s dominant geographic feature is a large inland sea surrounded by a temperate continental region. Warm climates, fertile coastal plains, island chains, and navigable rivers were deliberately selected to reproduce environmental conditions similar to the historical Mediterranean world.

Although Antonia contains multiple continents, the primary experiment occurs on a single continent surrounding the inland sea. This region hosts several seeded civilizations whose interaction recreates a complex ancient geopolitical system.

The Antonian Republic occupies a large peninsula projecting into the inland sea. The peninsula contains fertile plains, upland pastoral regions, and navigable rivers connecting inland settlements with maritime trade routes.

The capital of the republic developed along a major river several kilometers inland from the sea, occupying several hills overlooking the river valley. Temples and aristocratic residences dominate elevated terrain while markets, warehouses, and docks developed along the riverbanks.

Other seeded civilizations around the inland sea include Greek-style maritime city states and a powerful Egyptian-style river kingdom with a Ptolemaic Greek administrative elite. A Persian-style imperial civilization exists on another continent of the planet and initially interacts with the inland sea civilizations through long-distance maritime exploration.

IDEA-316
------------------------------------------------
Name:
Antonian Republic

Domain:
Factions

Parent Idea:
Antonia

Era:
Planetary Period

Tags:
republic
rome
senate
legions
roman_society

Description:
The Antonian Republic is the primary civilization seeded in the Antonia cultural experiment and represents a close reconstruction of the political and social structures of the Late Roman Republic.

The state is organized as a senatorial republic governed by aristocratic families whose members dominate the senate. The senate directs foreign policy, finances, and military command while elected magistrates exercise executive authority for limited terms.

Citizen assemblies formally elect magistrates and approve legislation, though political influence is heavily shaped by patronage networks maintained by elite families.

Society reproduces Roman republican social hierarchy including senatorial aristocracy, equestrian commercial elites, ordinary citizens, freedmen, and enslaved populations. Wealth and prestige are strongly tied to agricultural land ownership and maritime trade.

Military organization follows the Roman legionary model. Citizen legions are raised for campaigns and commanded by magistrates, while auxiliary forces recruited from allied communities provide cavalry and specialized troops. Successful generals gain enormous prestige and political influence, creating structural tensions between republican institutions and ambitious military leaders.

Religion follows the Roman civic religious system including temples, priesthoods, public festivals, and ritual ceremonies reinforcing the authority of the republic.

IDEA-317
------------------------------------------------
Name:
Mark Anthony Seed

Domain:
People

Parent Idea:
Antonia Experiment

Era:
Planetary Period

Tags:
person_seed
roman_republic
general
aristocrat

Description:
The Mark Anthony Seed is a Cultural Seeding Group personality template introduced into the Antonian Republic as part of the Antonia Experiment.

The template is based on the historical Roman figure Marcus Antonius and represents the archetype of a charismatic aristocratic military commander emerging from a declining noble lineage.

Individuals derived from this seed typically originate from established aristocratic families but grow up during periods of intense political rivalry. Their early lives often include excessive spending, political scandal, and debt before entering military service as a path to restore family prestige.

Within the Antonian Republic this personality pattern tends to produce ambitious political figures who gain popularity through military success, personal charisma, and patronage networks. Such individuals frequently become central actors in elite political competition and may accumulate personal loyalty from soldiers and clients.

The template was introduced to observe the emergence of powerful republican generals whose ambitions may challenge the stability of senatorial government.
