---

STEP 2 — orbit.py

KeplerOrbit:

* 2D elliptical orbits
* parent-based
* tick-driven

DynamicOrbit:

* position + velocity
* gravity from bodies

---

STEP 3 — object.py

SpaceObject:

* holds orbit
* resolves position
* optional mass (for gravity source)

---

STEP 4 — test_sim integration

Replace placeholder orbit logic with:

* star (no orbit)
* planet (Kepler orbit)
* optional spacecraft (dynamic orbit)

---

## CONSTRAINTS

* 2D only (no inclination yet)
* Tick-based time (not frame dt)

Hybrid orbit system:

* Kepler → planets
* Dynamic → spacecraft

Unidirectional gravity:

* planets affect spacecraft
* spacecraft do not affect planets

---

## MINIMAL PHYSICS SCOPE

* Newton gravity
* semi-implicit Euler integration
* no collisions
* no thrust

---

## EXPECTED RESULT

* planet orbits star (ellipse)
* optional spacecraft moves under gravity
* stable motion
* tick counter increases

DEVELOPMENT SESSION 2 (ARCHITECTURE DESIGN)
===========================================

---

## CORE DESIGN DIRECTION

Establish a layered, multi-simulation architecture with:

* parallel simulation instances
* tick-based global time
* time resolution depending on lowest active layer
* cross-layer interactions (person ↔ institution ↔ world)

---

## SIMULATION ARCHITECTURE

SimulationManager
├ active_simulations
├ global_tick
└ TimelineManager

Simulations run in parallel (tab-based):

* WorldSimulation
* ProducerSimulation (multiple instances)
* PersonSimulation

No simulation pauses others.

---

## TIME SYSTEM DESIGN

* Global tick system
* Later: real time units derived from tick

Time resolution rule:

* lowest active simulation determines tick scale

Example:

strategy → yearly
producer → minute
person → second

→ global tick = second

---

## TIMELINE SYSTEM (PLANNED)

Timeline
├ canon_events
└ simulation_events

* simulations emit events
* events recorded only on significant changes
* produces diverging world history

---

## PRODUCER SIMULATION DESIGN

ProducerSimulation
└ production_sites

ProductionSite
└ production_lines

ProductionLine
input_resources
output_resources
cycle_duration
workers_required
machines_required

---

## PRODUCTION MODEL

* discrete production cycles
* production happens on cycle completion

Example:

10 ticks → 1 output

---

## PLAYER-DRIVEN INDUSTRY

* production defined by production lines
* factories are containers

Player can:

* add/remove lines
* assign workers
* upgrade systems

---

## POP SYSTEM INTEGRATION (PLANNED)

Person → Worker POP

* production efficiency depends on workforce

---

## PERSON SIMULATION DESIGN

Person
id
position
job
current_location

* RTS-style movement
* interaction with institutions

---

## CROSS-LAYER INTERACTION

Person enters production site →
opens ProducerSimulation →
person becomes worker POP

---

## DEVELOPMENT ORDER (CONFIRMED)

1. Minimal tick engine

3. Timeline divergence system
4. Complex clock system
5. Person RTS simulation
