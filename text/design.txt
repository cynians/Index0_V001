INDEX_0
Design Document
Version 3 – Exploration, Snapshot & Simulation Architecture


========================================================
1. CORE CONCEPT
========================================================

Index_0 is a timeline exploration and simulation system structured as a game.

Players explore a historical knowledge graph composed of entities such as:

- people
- species
- vehicles
- factions
- places
- maps
- events
- organizations
- objects
- technologies
- production sites

The primary interface is a canvas of tiles representing entities.

Players explore the timeline, investigate entities, and perform
"simulation dives" into specific places, entities, or events.

Simulation results can influence the canonical timeline.

The system therefore operates as a hybrid of:

knowledge graph
historical simulation engine
world exploration game.



========================================================
2. CORE MODES
========================================================

The system operates in two main modes.

1. Exploration Mode
2. Simulation Mode



--------------------------------------------------------
2.1 Exploration Mode
--------------------------------------------------------

Exploration Mode represents the abstract historical world state.

It stores:

- entity states
- entity relationships
- maps
- regions
- events
- snapshot records

Entities exist as abstract representations.

Examples:

Species
→ geographic range
→ population estimate

Cities
→ population
→ institutions
→ industries

Vehicles
→ production presence
→ operator presence

People
→ location
→ occupation
→ relationships

Exploration Mode is not simulated.

It represents the canonical historical dataset.



--------------------------------------------------------
2.2 Simulation Mode
--------------------------------------------------------

Simulation Mode generates dynamic systems derived from exploration data.

Simulations instantiate agents derived from exploration entities.

Example transformations:

Species range
→ individual animals

City population
→ workers
→ vehicles
→ institutions

Vehicle types
→ individual vehicles

Simulation types include:

- ecosystem simulations
- military simulations
- vehicle simulations
- industrial simulations
- grand strategy simulations
- urban simulations

Simulation results may influence the timeline.



========================================================
3. TIME SYSTEM
========================================================

The timeline represents the global progression of the world state.

The timeline is continuous but is stored through discrete
historical snapshots.

The player may move the exploration state to any point in time.

The currently selected time determines:

- which entities exist
- which relationships are active
- which events are active
- which maps and regions exist
- which technologies exist

Entity existence is determined through:

start_year
end_year

Example:

Entity:

start_year = 2100
end_year = 2140

The entity appears only between these years.



--------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Active Timeline State
--------------------------------------------------------

At any moment the system maintains a single active world state.

Active world state includes:

active entities
active relationships
active regions
active technologies
active events

The active world state is reconstructed from the snapshot system.



========================================================
4. SNAPSHOT SYSTEM
========================================================

World history is stored in snapshots.

Snapshot model:

Hybrid snapshot system.

Full snapshots occur every 10 years.

Delta snapshots store changes between full snapshots.


Structure example:

Full Snapshot 2100
  ├ Delta 2102
  ├ Delta 2104
  ├ Delta 2108
Full Snapshot 2110


Snapshots store:

- entity states
- relationships
- map layers
- region heatmaps
- events
- technology states
- production states



--------------------------------------------------------
4.1 Snapshot Reconstruction
--------------------------------------------------------

To reconstruct a world state at time T:

1. Load the most recent full snapshot before T.
2. Apply all delta snapshots until T.
3. Apply entity time filters.
4. Apply relationship time filters.

This produces the active exploration state.



--------------------------------------------------------
4.2 Regional Snapshots
--------------------------------------------------------

Snapshots are stored per region.

Each map region has its own snapshot timeline.

Simulations operate primarily on a single region but may influence:

- neighboring regions
- parent regions
- contained subregions
- connected entities



--------------------------------------------------------
4.3 Snapshot Drift
--------------------------------------------------------

Simulations are guided toward the next known snapshot state.

Example:

2100 population = 2000
2110 population = 20000

During simulation, modifiers bias outcomes toward the target state.

The simulation is not random, but goal-directed toward the next snapshot.

========================================================
9. INSIGHT SYSTEM
========================================================

Insight is the main player resource.

Insight represents the player's ability to analyze and influence history.



Insight is gained by:

discovering entities

Insight is spent on:

revealing hidden relationships

========================================================
12. SIMULATION DERIVATION
========================================================

Simulations derive dynamic agents from exploration entities.

Agent generation uses hybrid statistical sampling.



Example:

Wolf population = 1200

Agents are temporary by default.



--------------------------------------------------------
12.1 Agent Promotion
--------------------------------------------------------

After simulation, the player may promote agents to entities.

Example:

soldier → historical figure
prototype vehicle → new vehicle entity
animal specimen → species entry

16. SIMULATION MAP SELECTION
========================================================

Simulation maps follow priority rules.

1. Use prebuilt simulation map if available.

Example:

Otherwise:

Europe map → crop region → derive map.

========================================================
X. CONFLICT SYSTEM
========================================================

The Conflict System represents organized violent confrontations
within the Index_0 simulation architecture.

Conflicts act as a structural bridge between the exploration
layer of the historical world state and the simulation systems
that resolve military and violent interactions.

Unlike simple historical events, conflicts are designed to
support multi-layer simulations that operate across different
levels of abstraction.

These layers allow the same conflict to be experienced from
multiple perspectives ranging from strategic command to
individual combat participation.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.1 Role in the Simulation Architecture
--------------------------------------------------------

The Conflict System does not directly simulate combat.
Instead it organizes the structural relationships between
entities involved in violent confrontations.

The actual resolution of conflicts occurs in specialized
simulation modules.

These modules operate on different layers of the simulation
architecture:

Strategic Layer
    CommanderSim

Operational / Tactical Layer
    WargameSim

Individual Combat Layer
    CombatSim

Conflicts therefore function primarily as a structural
framework which connects entities, locations, forces and
operations across these simulation systems.

Simulation outcomes modify the world state and may later
be canonized into the historical timeline.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.2 Conflict Entity Structure
--------------------------------------------------------

A Conflict represents a violent confrontation involving
multiple actors.

Conflicts may represent large wars, localized battles,
insurrections, pirate attacks, riots, or other forms of
organized violence.

Conflicts are stored as independent entities and are
linked to historical events rather than being implemented
as event subtypes.

Each conflict may contain or reference multiple operations
and engagements which together represent the unfolding
military situation.

Basic structure:

Conflict
    operations
    participants
    time range
    linked events

Operations represent the strategic layer of the conflict
while engagements represent tactical combat situations.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.3 Operations (Strategic Layer)
--------------------------------------------------------

Operations represent strategic military activity within
a conflict.

They operate on large-scale maps and involve military
formations executing coordinated plans.

Examples include:

- campaigns
- defensive operations
- fleet maneuvers
- system-scale invasions

Operations exist independently from conflicts and may
appear in multiple conflicts if necessary.

Each operation has its own time range and theatre.

Typical operation attributes include:

- strategic map or theatre
- commanders
- participating formations
- operational objectives
- time range



--------------------------------------------------------
X.4 Engagements (Tactical Layer)
--------------------------------------------------------

Engagements represent tactical military confrontations.

They are the primary interface between the strategic
operation layer and the battlefield simulation systems.

Engagements operate on smaller maps representing specific
battlefields such as:

- orbital debris fields
- planetary surfaces
- city districts
- industrial facilities

Engagements determine which formations and assets are
deployed in a particular battle.

The WargameSim module resolves the tactical outcome of
these engagements.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.5 Combat Situations (Individual Layer)
--------------------------------------------------------

At the lowest level of simulation, combat is resolved
through CombatSim.

Combat situations occur when individuals participate
directly in violent encounters.

These situations are derived from engagements and operate
within the person-layer simulation.

This allows players to experience the same conflict
through multiple perspectives.

Example viewpoints include:

Strategic Commander
    directing formations on a system map

Formation Officer
    executing orders during a fleet engagement

Individual Participant
    fighting in a battlefield combat situation

This layered approach allows conflicts to connect the
grand strategy simulation with the person-scale simulation
systems.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.6 Military Formations
--------------------------------------------------------

Military formations represent organized armed groups.

Formations are stored in a separate schema and may represent:

- naval fleets
- army units
- militias
- mercenary companies
- pirate flotillas
- security forces

Formations are hierarchical and may contain an unlimited
number of sub-units.

Example structure:

Formation
    units
        sub-units
            sub-units

Formations own assets such as vehicles and equipment.

Engagements reference which formations and assets are
deployed in a particular battle.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.7 Interaction with Other Systems
--------------------------------------------------------

The Conflict System interacts with multiple other systems
within the simulation architecture.

Important integrations include:

Logistics Systems
    supply networks for formations and operations

Economic Systems
    wartime industrial conversion and resource demand

Population Systems
    recruitment, displacement, and civilian effects

Doctrine Systems
    rules of engagement and strategic behavior

Snapshot System
    reusable simulation starting states

Conflicts therefore act as a coordination layer connecting
many independent simulation modules.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.8 Simulation Outcomes and Canonization
--------------------------------------------------------

Simulation modules produce outcomes which modify the
world state.

These outcomes may include:

- destruction of infrastructure
- loss of vehicles and assets
- territorial changes
- population displacement
- economic disruption

At the end of a simulation timeline the system evaluates
the resulting world state.

Players may spend points to reduce certain impacts or to
canonize specific outcomes into the historical timeline.

This mechanism allows the simulation layer to influence
the canonical history represented in Exploration Mode.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.9 Example: Oxyde Corporate War
--------------------------------------------------------

An example application of the Conflict System can be seen
in the Oxyde Corporate War.

During the late Planetary Period, corporate factions fought
for control of the industrial infrastructure of the
Oxyde System.

Major battles occurred in orbit around the planet Patina.

The destruction of shipyards and fleets during the war
produced the Patina Orbital Graveyard and its associated
wreckage rings.

These debris structures later became major salvage zones
and economic centers in the system.

Objects such as Patina OO-7-285 originate from these
conflicts and are recorded in the orbital object registry.



========================================================
X. PERSON LAYER SIMULATION
========================================================

The Person Layer represents the lowest level of
individual simulation within the Index_0 architecture.

At this layer, individual persons are simulated as
independent actors with identities, relationships,
knowledge, goals, and physical presence in the world.

The Person Layer connects large-scale systems such as
economy, conflict, culture, and politics to the
individual experiences of people within the simulation.

This allows the simulation to represent both large
historical processes and the personal perspectives of
individual participants.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.1 Selective Person Simulation
--------------------------------------------------------

Not all individuals in the world are simulated at the
same level of detail.

The simulation system uses selective fidelity depending
on the relevance of individuals to the current context.

Full person simulation is applied to:

- the currently active person
- individuals directly interacting with that person
- historically relevant or named individuals
- participants in active simulations

Other individuals remain simulated at higher abstraction
layers such as city-level population or military unit
composition.

For example, within a military formation only a limited
number of soldiers may exist as fully simulated persons,
while the remaining personnel are represented through
aggregate unit structures.

This selective approach allows the system to simulate
large populations without excessive computational cost.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.2 Knowledge-Based Simulation
--------------------------------------------------------

A key principle of the Person Layer is that simulation
fidelity depends on what the person knows.

Each person maintains an internal knowledge model
consisting of facts and beliefs.

Facts represent information known to be true.

Beliefs represent information that the person believes
to be true but which may be incorrect.

Because of this distinction, persons may operate with
incomplete or inaccurate knowledge of the world.

Entities unknown to the person are simulated only at
higher abstraction layers.

If a person becomes aware of an entity, that entity may
be promoted to a higher level of simulation fidelity.

Historical snapshots of individuals may also exist.
When these snapshots are replayed within the simulation
they appear as ghost behaviors reproducing the canonical
actions of that individual at that time.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.3 Person Core State
--------------------------------------------------------

Each person contains several major state layers.

Identity Layer

- name
- background
- affiliations
- biography

Knowledge Layer

- known facts
- beliefs
- memories

Capability Layer

- attributes
- skills
- experience

World State Layer

- location
- inventory
- finances
- current activity

Social Layer

- relationships
- general opinions of others
- cultural affiliations

Together these layers determine how a person perceives
and interacts with the world.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.4 Memory and Timeline Integration
--------------------------------------------------------

Person memory is implemented through the snapshot
system of the simulation timeline.

Snapshots capture world states at specific points in
time.

A person's life history can therefore be reconstructed
through the sequence of snapshots in which they appear.

This mechanism enables:

- replay of historical states
- branching simulation timelines
- reconstruction of past events
- ghost replay of canonical behaviors



--------------------------------------------------------
X.5 Relationships and Social Networks
--------------------------------------------------------

Persons are connected through asymmetric relationship
links.

Each relationship includes:

- relationship type
- relationship strength

Examples of relationship types include:

- family
- friend
- colleague
- employer
- rival
- enemy
- subordinate

Relationships are asymmetric, meaning each individual
may perceive the relationship differently.

Relationship strength evolves through interaction and
shared experiences.

These relationship networks form the basis for social
dynamics, cooperation, and conflict.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.6 General Opinion Network
--------------------------------------------------------

Reputation is not represented as a single global value.

Instead, every person maintains a general opinion of
other individuals.

General opinion values are influenced by:

- direct interactions
- relationship history
- rumors
- cultural biases
- faction affiliations
- legal records

Global reputation emerges from the distributed network
of these opinions rather than from a centralized score.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.7 Traits, Emotions, and Goals
--------------------------------------------------------

Person behavior is influenced by a combination of
stable traits, emotional states, and dynamic goals.

Traits represent relatively stable psychological
characteristics such as curiosity, aggression,
discipline, or risk tolerance.

Traits may slowly evolve through life experiences.

Emotions represent short-term psychological states
such as fear, anger, stress, or morale.

Goals represent dynamic motivations that influence
decision making and behavior.

Daily schedules normally guide behavior, but goals
may override scheduled activities when circumstances
require.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.8 Skills, Education, and Experience
--------------------------------------------------------

Persons possess skills which may improve through both
experience and training.

Skills may be developed through:

- practical use
- formal education
- apprenticeships
- military training
- self-directed learning

Educational institutions provide structured learning
environments with defined curricula.

Education systems therefore serve as a bridge between
the person layer and the broader technological and
industrial development of societies.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.9 Health, Aging, and Mortality
--------------------------------------------------------

The health system models detailed physical conditions.

Persons possess body-part based injury states,
medical conditions, and recovery processes.

Injuries may occur through accidents, disease, or
combat situations.

Medical treatment, natural healing, or prosthetic
augmentation may influence recovery.

Aging is simulated throughout a person's life.

Age influences:

- physical capabilities
- health risks
- skill acquisition
- mortality probability.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.10 Economy and Personal Finances
--------------------------------------------------------

Persons participate directly in the economic system.

Each person maintains personal finances including:

- cash
- bank accounts
- assets
- debts
- income streams

Employment links persons to workplaces within the
production system.

Production itself can occur independently of individual
persons through abstract labor models, but fully
simulated persons may occupy worker positions and
influence production outcomes.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.11 Housing and Living Arrangements
--------------------------------------------------------

Housing entities represent buildings and residential
units.

Each housing unit contains residents and may have an
owner.

Housing systems interact with:

- personal finances
- family structures
- urban population simulation
- migration and displacement

Conflicts or disasters may destroy housing, forcing
residents to relocate.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.12 Movement and Transportation
--------------------------------------------------------

Person movement is modeled through route-based travel.

In abstract simulation states, travel occurs along
routes over time.

When a relevant simulation is active, the person is
placed on a detailed map and movement is simulated
with precise spatial coordinates.

Persons may own or access vehicles which function as
independent entities within the vehicle registry.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.13 Culture and Social Institutions
--------------------------------------------------------

Culture influences values, practices, and belief
systems.

Persons may belong to cultural groups but interpret
cultural practices individually.

Individuals may strongly follow, partially follow,
reinterpret, or reject cultural norms.

Leisure activities, entertainment institutions, and
social gatherings provide spaces where relationships
form and cultural practices are reinforced.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.14 Law, Crime, and Enforcement
--------------------------------------------------------

Legal systems are defined by factions and jurisdictions.

Criminal actions occur when individuals violate
established laws.

Enforcement depends on whether authorities become aware
of the offense.

Detection may occur through witnesses, surveillance,
reports, or investigation.

Legal consequences may include fines, imprisonment,
exile, or other punishments depending on the governing
system.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.15 Political Participation
--------------------------------------------------------

Persons may participate in political systems when the
political structure of a faction allows it.

Political participation may include:

- voting
- holding office
- organizing movements
- influencing policy

Political systems vary between factions and may range
from democratic assemblies to corporate or authoritarian
structures.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.16 Conflict Participation
--------------------------------------------------------

Persons may participate in conflicts at multiple levels.

Individuals may appear as:

- soldiers within military formations
- officers commanding units
- civilians affected by warfare
- participants in battlefield combat situations

CombatSim handles individual combat encounters derived
from engagements within the broader conflict system.



--------------------------------------------------------
X.17 Death and Legacy
--------------------------------------------------------

When a person dies, the individual is not removed from
the historical record.

Instead, the death event is recorded within the timeline.

Consequences of death may include:

- inheritance of assets
- continuation of family lines
- memory persistence in other persons
- historical documentation

The timeline system preserves the individual's legacy,
allowing past persons to remain part of the evolving
historical narrative.
